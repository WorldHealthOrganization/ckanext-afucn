{{ _('What are Locations?') }}

{% trans %} Locations help organize health-related datasets by the places they relate to, such as countries, provinces, regions, or localities. By categorizing health data based on geographic areas, you can make it easier for users to find datasets relevant to specific locations, which is vital for improving public health outcomes. {% endtrans %}

{% trans %} For example: {% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}
    National health data: Datasets related to the health of an entire country, such as national disease statistics, health expenditure, or overall life expectancy trends. {% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}
    Regional health initiatives: Datasets that focus on the health challenges of specific regions, like access to healthcare, regional vaccination rates, or public health policies. {% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}
    Locality-specific health datasets: Datasets relevant to urban and rural localities, such as air quality measurements, disease outbreaks in towns or villages, or local healthcare services availability. {% endtrans %}
